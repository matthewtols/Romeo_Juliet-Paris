Paris

Paris's Character traits:

  • In love with Juliet
  • Determined to protect Juliet
  • More of a 'behind the scenes' person
  • Determined to marry Juliet

Where Paris appears in each Act

Act 1

Scene 1

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 2

Paris is introduced briefly in this scene. He is trying to convince Capulet, Juliet's father, to let him marry Juliet. He is turned down.

Scene 3

In this scene, Paris is talked of by Juliet's mother, Juliet's Nurse and Juliet. Lady Capulet is informing Juliet of Paris' desire for them to get married.

Scene 4

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 5

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Act 2

Scene 1

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 2

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 3

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 4

Paris is briefly mentioned by Juliet's Nurse in this scene.

Scene 5

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 6

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Act 3

Scene 1

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 2

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 3

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 4

Paris is once again seen in this scene trying to convince Capulet to let him marry Romeo. This time however, he is successfull and they decide to have them married on the Thursday.

Scene 5

In this scene, Paris is mentioned and talked of by Capulet, Lady Capulet and Juliet. Capulet and Lady Capulet are informing Juliet of the marriage coming up.

Act 4

Scene 1

In this scene, Paris and Juliet accidentally meet at Friar Lawrence's Cell. Juliet tells him that she is not happy to be soon married to him. He ignores her. He was at Friar Lawrence's Cell to tell Friar Lawrence of the marriage.

Scene 2

Paris does not appear in this scene other than being mentioned by Capulet.

Scene 3

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 4

Paris is briefly mentioned by Juliet's Nurse before entering the scene and seeing Juliet "dead". He expresses his laments before the scene ends.

Act 5

Scene 1

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 2

Paris does not appear in this scene.

Scene 3

In the climax scene, Paris opens up the narration by coming to lament Juliet's "death". When Romeo appears, they both duel. Paris is slain and here after only talked about.
